What is The 16-0 Game?

The 16-0 Game is the college football perfect season game — the college football version of 82-0. A perfect 16-0 season has happened exactly twice in major college football history: Yale in 1894, and Indiana in 2025-26, the first season it was even possible under the 12-team College Football Playoff (12 regular-season games, a conference championship, a first-round bye, and three Playoff wins). This game asks one question: with an all-time roster, can you do it?

It's a free daily draft game you play in your browser. Each pick puts a random program-season on the clock — Alabama 2020, USC 2004, Miami 2001, Nebraska 1995 — and you draft their best quarterback, running back, receivers, offensive line, and a defensive captain from thousands of real player seasons (through 2025, plus hand-rated legends like Herschel Walker, Barry Sanders, Bo Jackson, and Charles Woodson). Your defensive captain's position changes how your defense plays: a cornerback shuts down passing attacks, a defensive lineman stuffs the run, a linebacker defends both.

When your roster is set, simulate a full 12-game season, your conference championship, and the College Football Playoff. Win every game and you've matched Yale 1894 and Indiana 2025 — perfect and immortal. Your star can win the Heisman along the way. A new daily challenge drops every day with the same teams for everyone, so you can share your score and compete with friends on the daily leaderboard.
